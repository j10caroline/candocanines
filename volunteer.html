VOLUNTEER

As a Puppy Program volunteer for Can Do Canines, you transform our mission from words on paper to a priceless reality for people with disabilities. No previous experience is required, and we are extremely grateful for all of our volunteers.

PUPPY PROGRAM

PUPPY RAISERS
When a puppy enters the Can Do Canines training program, it may be anywhere from 8 weeks to 12 months of age. The puppy needs a dedicated person or family to raise them until they are 18 months old, which is when they come back to our facility to be trained.
GREAT START HOMES
As a Great Start volunteer you’ll host a puppy for approximately 6 to 8 weeks. During this time you help prepare puppies to be raised in one of our prison programs by providing them with early socialization, basic obedience, potty and crate training.
SHORT TERM FOSTERS
Short Term Fosters provide temporary care as needed for the dogs in training. This can range from a few days to a few weeks. The dogs placed with these volunteers are usually between 5 months to 2 years of age.
LONG TERM FOSTERS
Long Term Fosters are volunteers who care for a dog in training for 1-6 months. These dogs are usually 1 year or older and may have been trained in a prison program or with a Puppy Raiser. Long term fosters help give dogs in training a variety of experiences.
PRISON FOSTERS
Prison Puppy Foster Home Volunteers take the dogs out of the prison environment for an average of four days at a time, preferably twice a month. Pick up and drop off is handled on prison grounds, but there is no contact with inmates.
